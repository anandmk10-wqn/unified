Our Mission

We are driven by a passion to empower families. Through advocacy, education, and community-building, we strive to create a world where every child with special needs has the opportunity to thrive, and every parent feels supported and valued on their unique journey. At Unified Needs, we believe no child or family should ever feel alone or overlooked. We see you, we hear you, and we are here to walk alongside you. Our mission is to create a compassionate circle of support that embraces the complete educational, emotional, and social well-being of every child. We are a dedicated partner for families, schools, and local services, committed to building bridges where barriers exist. By fostering understanding and connection, we turn exclusion into engagement and pave the way for bright, positive futures. Our ultimate hope is to see every child thrive back in a mainstream school setting.

Our Vision

To unify the educational, emotional, and social needs of children and families under one inclusive, responsive, and compassionate support framework. Unified Needs aspires to become a trusted partner to local schools, services, and families helping to reduce exclusion, increase engagement, and promote long-term positive outcomes for children who are often overlooked by traditional systems.

Our ultimate aim is reintegration: Wherever possible, we work towards reintroducing children into mainstream school settings without isolating the family or the student. We recognise that carers need a break too, and our service is designed to support the whole family while preparing the child for a successful return to school life.

About Us

Founded by parents who have walked this path, 'A Place of Hope' was born from a desire to create the resource we wished we had. Our team is composed of dedicated caregivers, professionals, and volunteers committed to providing a beacon of light and a network of strength for families everywhere. Unified Needs was born from lived experience. The director is a parent of four neurodivergent children and has firsthand knowledge of the challenges of navigating SEN and AP systems. This insight underpins our approach: grounded in empathy, realism, and action. We provide a flexible and community-based model that bridges the gap between education, family support, and wellbeing placing children and families at the centre of everything we do. Unified Needs provides flexible, tailored support through a range of services that prioritise emotional safety, academic progress, and family involvement. We are ready to collaborate with local authority teams, education settings, SEND services, and Early Help to ensure our model enhances not replaces existing provision.

Unified Needs is a newly established alternative provision (AP) service in Cambridgeshire, created to address the needs of children and young people who are unable to thrive within traditional mainstream education. Our service is designed to offer personalised academic, emotional, and behavioural support for pupils at risk of exclusion, those with special educational needs (SEN), or those experiencing other barriers to learning and engagement. What makes us different: Unlike many other services, students can be referred to Unified Needs without requiring an EHCP, IEP, ILP, or completion of the APDR cycle. This unique flexibility allows schools and families to access immediate, high-quality support without waiting for lengthy assessments or paperwork.
